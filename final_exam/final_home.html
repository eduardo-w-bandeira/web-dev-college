The Celestial Disk

A celestial disk serves as a practical tool for astronomers and stargazers alike. It comprises a pair of disks: The first disk showcases an array of discernible constellations within the nighttime expanse, while the second overlay houses a window that precisely designates the currently observable sky sector. This secondary disk is then turned to align with the exact date and time in question. Various sizes and types of celestial disks are available. The key consideration is ensuring a celestial disk that corresponds to the longitude of your stargazing location.

Displayed on the right is an internet-based celestial disk. This rendition involves the superimposition of two images. The uppermost image represents the observing window of the celestial disk, while the underlying image depicts the celestial map. This celestial disk is automated, effortlessly adjusting to display the present date, time, and discernible constellations for individuals located at a longitude of 40° North. To employ a celestial disk, hold it directly above with the arrow oriented northward, as indicated on the observing disk.
